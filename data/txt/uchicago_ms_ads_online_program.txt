Online Program | DSI

Program overview:
The Online MS in Applied Data Science program is an online data science master’s degree designed to align with the demands of working professionals while maintaining live, interactive coursework.

Academic format:
- Structured online learning experience
- Live classes designed for working professionals
- Available full-time or part-time
- Full-time and part-time students begin in Autumn quarter
- Students benefit from the same faculty, peer group, and academic rigor as the in-person program

Program leadership:
- Online Program Director: Arnab Bose, PhD

Career and support:
The page emphasizes rigorous classes, expert instructors, leading-edge technology, and a strong professional network.
Students have access to:
- a full-service team dedicated to the MS in Applied Data Science program
- tailored in-house career services
- academic advising
- networking opportunities in person and virtually
- student affairs, career services, and instructional services support

Student experience:
The Online program is described as supportive for students who may also be working full-time.
Staff leading the online program have backgrounds in instructional design and best practices for online learning.

Alumni access:
Online students have access to program alumni through:
- Immersion Weekend events
- virtual career services programs

Graduates also join the broader:
- Physical Sciences Division alumni network
- UChicago alumni network

Schedule and delivery format:
The online format combines:
- live synchronous classes
- structured asynchronous coursework

Synchronous classes:
- Weekly real-time classes with instructors and peers
- Scheduled on weekday evenings at 6:00 PM CT
- Occasional Saturdays at 9:00 AM CT
- Students admitted into the online program are expected to attend the live sessions

Asynchronous coursework:
- Lecture videos
- Readings
- Discussions
- Project work
- Group collaboration
- Assignments completed around personal and professional commitments

In-person Immersion Weekend:
Students have the opportunity to attend an in-person Immersion Weekend in Chicago in the Autumn quarter.

Immersion Weekend activities may include:
- networking with industry leaders and alumni
- meeting faculty and classmates
- connecting with peers from across the country
- participating in interactive sessions focused on industry solutions
- gaining career insights and discussing AI/ML trends and challenges

Curriculum structure:
Students complete:
- 12 courses total
  - 6 core courses
  - 4 elective courses
  - 2 Capstone courses
- a tailored Career Seminar (required, noncredit)

Program pacing:
- Part-time students enroll in 2 courses per quarter and usually take classes in the evenings or on Saturdays
- Full-time students take 3 courses per quarter
- Some full-time courses may be offered during the day

Curriculum philosophy:
The curriculum is described as rigorous and reviewed annually to keep pace with the evolving data science landscape.

Foundational courses (noncredit, optional):
Foundational courses are designed and taught by MS in Applied Data Science faculty and instructors.
They are available at no additional cost.

Required assessment:
- Beginning in academic year 2024–25, all entering students complete a required online Foundational Skills Assessment
- Students may opt out of taking the assessment by registering for all four Foundational courses instead

Timing of foundational courses:
- Introduction to Statistical Concepts and R are pre-quarter courses during the 5 weeks leading up to the first quarter
- Python for Data Science runs during the first five weeks of the first quarter
- Advanced Linear Algebra for Machine Learning runs during the second five weeks of the first quarter
- All Foundational courses are completed virtually for both In-Person and Online students

Career Seminar (required, noncredit):
The multi-quarter Career Seminar supports development of:
- industry professional skills
- job and internship search skills
- communication and other employer-valued competencies

The page notes:
- students enroll in the Career Seminar each quarter
- it is Pass/Fail
- it costs nothing and carries 0 units
- students with significant, relevant full-time work experience may be eligible to waive this requirement

Core courses (6):
1. Time Series Analysis and Forecasting
2. Statistical Models for Data Science
3. Machine Learning I
4. Machine Learning II
5. Data Engineering Platforms for Analytics
   or Big Data and Cloud Computing
6. Leadership and Consulting for Data Science

Capstone (2):
The required Capstone Project:
- is completed over 2 quarters
- covers research design, implementation, and writing
- starts in the third quarter for full-time students
- generally starts two quarters before graduation for part-time students
- may be industry-focused or research-focused

Additional core/capstone wording shown later on the page:
- Full-time students start the capstone in their third quarter
- Part-time students generally begin the capstone in their fifth quarter
