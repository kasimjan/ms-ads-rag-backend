Page title:
Course Progressions | DSI

Overview:
The page provides an interactive sample course schedule for the In-Person and Online MS in Applied Data Science program offerings.

Flexible formats:
- Full-time and part-time options are available in both the Online and In-Person formats.
- Full-time students take 3 classes per quarter (300 units).
- Part-time students take 2 classes per quarter (200 units).
- A sample schedule for the MBA/MS is available on the Booth website.
- Course offerings are subject to change.
- The Career Seminar is Pass/Fail and required for all students unless eligible to waive.
- There is no tuition or fees for the Career Seminar.
- Optional Foundational noncredit courses are available at no additional cost.

Curriculum details:
The page states that the MS in Applied Data Science program has two curriculum pathways:
1. 12-course program
2. 18-course thesis track

Both pathways include:
- core courses
- electives
- a multi-quarter Capstone Project
- a required Career Seminar

Additional thesis-track element:
- The 18-course program includes additional electives and a thesis requirement.

Foundational courses (optional, noncredit):
- Designed and taught by MS in Applied Data Science faculty and instructors
- Available at no additional cost
- Beginning in academic year 2024-25, all entering students complete a required online Foundational Skills Assessment
- Students may opt out of the assessment by registering for all four Foundational courses
- Introduction to Statistical Concepts and R are pre-quarter courses held during the 5 weeks leading up to the first quarter
- All Foundational courses are completed virtually for all students, regardless of In-Person or Online enrollment

Foundational course list:
1. Introduction to Statistical Concepts
2. R for Data Science
3. Python for Data Science
4. Advanced Linear Algebra for Machine Learning

Career Seminar (required, noncredit):
- A multi-quarter seminar focused on communication, collaboration, and ethical problem-solving
- Students with 3+ years of relevant full-time work experience may petition for a waiver
- 0 units, no cost

Core courses:
All students complete 6 core courses:
1. Statistical Models for Data Science
2. Leadership and Consulting for Data Science
3. Data Engineering Platforms for Analytics or Big Data and Cloud Computing
4. Machine Learning I
5. Time Series Analysis and Forecasting
6. Machine Learning II

Elective courses:
- Students in the 12-course track complete 4 electives
- Students in the 18-course thesis track complete 8 electives and 2 independent study courses
- Past electives listed on the page include:
  - Generative AI Principles
  - Advanced Computer Vision with Deep Learning
  - Advanced Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence
  - Bayesian Machine Learning with GenAI Applications
  - Data Science for Algorithmic Marketing
  - Data Visualization Techniques
  - Digital Marketing Analytics in Theory and Practice
  - Financial Analytics / Quantitative Finance
  - Health Analytics
  - Machine Learning Operations
  - Natural Language Processing and Cognitive Computing
  - Real Time Intelligent Systems
  - Reinforcement Learning
  - Supply Chain Optimization

Capstone project:
- Required 2-quarter Capstone Project
- Students work in teams on a real industry or research problem
- Full-time students typically begin Capstone in their third quarter
- Part-time students begin two quarters before graduation
