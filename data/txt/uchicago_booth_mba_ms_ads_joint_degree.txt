MBA/MS in Applied Data Science | The University of Chicago Booth School of Business

Program overview:
The MBA/MS in Applied Data Science is a joint degree at the intersection of business and technology.
The page says the program combines resources from:
- Chicago Booth
- the Data Science Institute
- the Physical Sciences Division

It is designed for students who want preparation across:
- data-driven business leadership
- analytics
- AI / machine learning
- management in data-centric environments

Format:
- Booth courses are in person
- MS in Applied Data Science courses are available online and in person
- In-person MS courses are held at NBC Tower in downtown Chicago
- The page says most students complete both degrees in 7 quarters, the same time it takes to earn the MBA alone

Program structure:
Students complete the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses, including:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- LEAD (Leadership Effectiveness and Development)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

- Tuition is assessed through Chicago Booth at a flat rate for six quarters of enrollment
- Pricing differs from Booth’s standard Full-Time MBA program
- Final tuition amounts are made available in the spring
- Students taking more than 2300 units are assessed for additional units

- Research-focused capstone project designed for the joint degree

- Done with a real client

Booth coursework:

LEAD:
Leadership Effectiveness and Development is required for all Full-Time MBA students in Autumn quarter of the first year and focuses on:
- self-awareness
- teamwork
- influence
- conflict management
- interpersonal communication
- presentation skills
- leadership development planning

Statistics note:
- Students are encouraged to take Applied Regression Analysis to complete the statistics foundation requirement
- It is not required
- Students who do not take 41100 may waive the statistics foundation requirement and instead take 100 additional units of Booth electives

Additional Booth coursework:
- Functions, Leadership & Management, and the Business Environment: 700 course units
- Electives: 400 course units, or 500 if the statistics foundation requirement is waived

Admissions process:
Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process.

Application instructions on the page:
- Complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application
- Select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest
- Complete the MBA/MS program supplement within the Booth application
- For full consideration, both the MBA application and the joint degree supplement must be completed in the same application round before submission

Testing:
- GMAT or GRE is required for the joint program
- International applicants may be required to submit proof of English proficiency

English proficiency minimums listed:
- TOEFL iBT: 104 minimum
- IELTS: 7 minimum

Eligibility:
- Students must be admitted to and begin both joint programs in the same academic year
- Students who have already started either the MS or the MBA are not eligible for the joint degree
