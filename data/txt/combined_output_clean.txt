UChicago DSI — MS in Applied Data Science — How to Apply
Source: https://datascience.uchicago.edu/education/masters-programs/ms-in-applied-data-science/how-to-apply/

How to Apply

The application for entrance in 2026 is now open.

Master’s in Applied Data Science Application Requirements

- The application portal for 2026 entrance is open.
- The In-Person and Online program admits full- and part-time students for entrance in the autumn quarter annually.
- Only completed applications are sent for committee review.
- The application portal may close early if the cohort is filled.

Next Application Deadlines

In-Person — Autumn 2026
- January 26, 2026 — International Application Deadline (requiring visa sponsorship from UChicago) [passed]
- March 4, 2026 — Second Priority Application Deadline [passed]
- May 6, 2026 — Third Priority Application Deadline
- June 23, 2026 — Final Application Deadline

Online — Autumn 2026
- June 23, 2026 — Final Application Deadline

Important Notes
- International applicants who require a new visa and did not apply by the January 2026 deadline must plan to apply in the next application cycle for Autumn 2027 entry.
- Applicants currently studying in the United States on an F-1 visa and transferring their SEVIS record from another U.S. institution may still apply by the remaining application deadlines.

Letters of Recommendation
- Two letters of recommendation are required.
- The program strongly recommends that at least one letter be written by a direct manager/supervisor or internship supervisor who can attest to workplace skills such as leadership, teamwork, collaboration, initiative, and management.
- Letters of recommendation can be uploaded after the application is submitted.
- Recommended sources: professors, supervisors/managers, professional mentors.
- Letters from family members, friends, or peers are not accepted.

Candidate Statement
- The candidate statement is a key part of the application.
- The application includes a prompt with detailed instructions.
- Best practices:
  - Do not restate your resume.
  - Do not exceed 700 words.

Resume/CV
- The admissions committee reviews the resume carefully.
- Important to include:
  - Work experience, including internships and part-time positions in analytics/data science
  - Major accomplishments and awards
  - Educational background, including areas of study
  - Professional conferences attended
- Not needed:
  - Undergraduate or graduate GPA

Programming Supplement
- The application includes a section on programming skills.
- Applicants with limited skills in R or Python can still be accepted, but prior programming experience is valued.
- Submit a PDF of no more than two pages demonstrating current programming language skills.
- Example: a PDF of work completed in R or Python.
- The programming supplement must be shared as a PDF file.
- Example qualities they may look for in code:
  - Ingest data from csv
  - Manage different data types
  - Use your function for data analysis
  - Visualize data
  - Wrangle data
  - Write your own function
  - Import libraries

Virtual Portfolio
- Short recorded videos are required within the application.
- One video prompt response is required; another is optional.
- Each video should be approximately 1 minute 30 seconds.

International Students
- Only the full-time, in-person program is visa-eligible.
- International students may also apply to the online program, which does not require citizenship or permanent resident status and does not provide visa sponsorship.

English Language Requirement
- Applicants who do not meet the English Language Proficiency criteria must submit proof of English language proficiency.
- Minimum scores listed on the page:
  - TOEFL: 102 (prior requirement) or 5 (current requirement; no subscore requirement)
  - IELTS: 7 (no subscore requirement)
- Waiver policy is referenced on the University of Chicago Graduate Affairs page.

Transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended
- Only unofficial transcripts are required at the application stage.
- If admitted, official transcripts must be sent.
- A bachelor’s degree is required to apply.
- E-transcripts should be sent directly to:
  applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu
- If documents cannot be sent electronically, mail them to:
  The University of Chicago
  Attention: MS in Applied Data Science Admissions
  455 N Cityfront Plaza Dr., Suite 2800
  Chicago, Illinois 60611
- Mailed documents must arrive in the original, school-sealed envelope or they will be considered unofficial.
- If the transcript does not show degree conferral information, an attested copy of the degree certificate is also required.

Application Fee
- $90 non-refundable application fee.
- For fee waiver questions, refer to the Physical Sciences Division fee waiver policy.

In-Person Program | DSI

Program overview:
The In-Person MS in Applied Data Science program is designed for students who learn best in an in-person classroom environment, want access to in-person student services and amenities, and prefer to be located in Chicago for the duration of the degree program.

Tailor Your Data Science Journey:
The In-Person MS in Applied Data Science program at the University of Chicago prepares students to work at the intersection of data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence through rigorous, applied coursework and real-world projects.

Study format:
- Available full-time or part-time.
- Many in-person students are early in their careers.
- The most competitive applicants are noted as typically having at least 1 year of full-time work experience and 1 or more relevant, sustained internships.
- For those seeking part-time study and those with more years of full-time work experience (3+), the online program is described as optimized for them.

1-Year 12 Course Program (Full- and Part-Time Options):
The signature 1-year, 12-course program is intended for students who want to fast-track their graduate studies.
- Full-time students typically graduate in 4–5 quarters.
- Part-time students may take up to 6 quarters.
- All students must graduate within 4 years maximum.

All students in this pathway complete:
1. 12 courses total:
   - 6 Core
   - 4 Electives
   - 2 Capstone
2. A required Career Seminar throughout the program.
   - Students with 3+ years of full-time work experience may petition for exemption.
3. A 2-quarter Capstone Project with a real industry partner.
   - Some students choose to complete a research Capstone.

Course scheduling:
- Courses are primarily offered in the evenings and on weekends to support working professionals.
- The program is STEM/OPT eligible.

2-Year Thesis Track, 18 Course Program (Full-Time Only):
Beginning in academic year 2026–27, UChicago offers an additional 2-year (21 month) pathway for in-person, full-time applicants.
- The application for the 2-Year Thesis Track (Autumn 2026 start) closed on December 4, 2025.
- This 2-year (21 month) pathway is completed over 6 academic quarters.
- The summer between years 1 and 2 serves as a vacation quarter.

This pathway is intended for students who:
- want a longer master’s program,
- want more elective courses,
- and have the capacity to complete a master’s thesis.

All students in this pathway complete:
1. 18 total courses:
   - 6 Core
   - 8 Electives
   - 2 Capstone
   - 2 Independent Study
2. A required Career Seminar throughout the program.
   - Students with 3+ years of full-time work experience may petition for exemption.
3. A 2-quarter Capstone Project.
4. A Master’s Thesis:
   - either a traditional written thesis
   - or a project-based thesis

STEM designation note:
This program is listed as a STEM designated degree by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security for purposes of the STEM OPT extension for eligible students. The page notes that approval of STEM OPT is at the discretion of U.S. Citizenship & Immigration Services.

Your Career Success:

The full-time, in-person MS in Applied Data Science program is described as STEM/OPT eligible.

Your Engagement:

Classroom locations:

Student services and amenities:
Students have access to tailored, in-person student services and program amenities.

Typical course times:

- This schedule is intended to allow students to work in an internship and/or job during the program.
- Select courses are offered during the day.

Your Student Experience:
In-person students have access to:
- expert faculty and instructors with industry expertise,
- a full-service student affairs team,

Program leadership:

Career Outcomes in Data Science:

Online Program | DSI

Program overview:
The Online MS in Applied Data Science program is an online data science master’s degree designed to align with the demands of working professionals while maintaining live, interactive coursework.

Academic format:
- Structured online learning experience
- Live classes designed for working professionals
- Available full-time or part-time
- Full-time and part-time students begin in Autumn quarter
- Students benefit from the same faculty, peer group, and academic rigor as the in-person program

Program leadership:
- Online Program Director: Arnab Bose, PhD

Career and support:
The page emphasizes rigorous classes, expert instructors, leading-edge technology, and a strong professional network.
Students have access to:
- a full-service team dedicated to the MS in Applied Data Science program
- tailored in-house career services
- academic advising
- networking opportunities in person and virtually
- student affairs, career services, and instructional services support

Student experience:
The Online program is described as supportive for students who may also be working full-time.
Staff leading the online program have backgrounds in instructional design and best practices for online learning.

Alumni access:
Online students have access to program alumni through:
- Immersion Weekend events
- virtual career services programs

Graduates also join the broader:
- Physical Sciences Division alumni network
- UChicago alumni network

Schedule and delivery format:
The online format combines:
- live synchronous classes
- structured asynchronous coursework

Synchronous classes:
- Weekly real-time classes with instructors and peers
- Scheduled on weekday evenings at 6:00 PM CT
- Occasional Saturdays at 9:00 AM CT
- Students admitted into the online program are expected to attend the live sessions

Asynchronous coursework:
- Lecture videos
- Readings
- Discussions
- Project work
- Group collaboration
- Assignments completed around personal and professional commitments

In-person Immersion Weekend:
Students have the opportunity to attend an in-person Immersion Weekend in Chicago in the Autumn quarter.

Immersion Weekend activities may include:
- networking with industry leaders and alumni
- meeting faculty and classmates
- connecting with peers from across the country
- participating in interactive sessions focused on industry solutions
- gaining career insights and discussing AI/ML trends and challenges
- hearing from students about Capstone Projects
- touring the Data Science Institute

  - Thursday: Evening Welcome and Social
  - Friday: Full Day Program TBA
  - Saturday: Full Day Program TBA

International student note for immersion weekend:
If students are traveling from outside the U.S. for Immersion Weekend, the page advises that they are encouraged to come in visitor/tourist status, using:
- B-1/B-2 visa
- or ESTA clearance, depending on citizenship / visa waiver eligibility

The page notes that:
- immersion days are optional
- they do not carry course enrollment
- students should be clear at entry that the visit is not for credit-bearing classes but for meeting classmates and participating as a visitor

Curriculum structure:
Students complete:
- 12 courses total
  - 6 core courses
  - 4 elective courses
  - 2 Capstone courses
- a tailored Career Seminar (required, noncredit)

Program pacing:
- Part-time students enroll in 2 courses per quarter and usually take classes in the evenings or on Saturdays
- Full-time students take 3 courses per quarter
- Some full-time courses may be offered during the day

Curriculum philosophy:
The curriculum is described as rigorous and reviewed annually to keep pace with the evolving data science landscape.

Foundational courses (noncredit, optional):
Foundational courses are designed and taught by MS in Applied Data Science faculty and instructors.
They are available at no additional cost.

Required assessment:
- Beginning in academic year 2024–25, all entering students complete a required online Foundational Skills Assessment
- Students may opt out of taking the assessment by registering for all four Foundational courses instead

Timing of foundational courses:
- Introduction to Statistical Concepts and R are pre-quarter courses during the 5 weeks leading up to the first quarter
- Python for Data Science runs during the first five weeks of the first quarter
- Advanced Linear Algebra for Machine Learning runs during the second five weeks of the first quarter
- All Foundational courses are completed virtually for both In-Person and Online students

   - optional
   - 0 units
   - no cost

   - 0 units

Career Seminar (required, noncredit):
The multi-quarter Career Seminar supports development of:
- industry professional skills
- job and internship search skills
- communication and other employer-valued competencies

The page notes:
- students enroll in the Career Seminar each quarter
- it is Pass/Fail

Capstone (2):
The required Capstone Project:
- is completed over 2 quarters
- covers research design, implementation, and writing
- starts in the third quarter for full-time students
- generally starts two quarters before graduation for part-time students
- may be industry-focused or research-focused

Electives:

Q: When will I receive my Master's in Applied Data Science admission decision?
A: Admissions decisions are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Only completed applications are reviewed.

Q: Does the admissions office allow recommenders to email their letter directly as an attachment to be included in an applicant’s file?
A: No. Recommenders must upload their letter of support using the URL sent to them electronically by the online application system.

Q: Do I need to provide my recommenders with instructions?
A: No. Recommendation forms and instructions are sent electronically to recommenders once their names are entered within the online application.

Q: My recommender did not receive notification, can I resend it?
A: Yes. The applicant can resend the link through the online application or ask the recommender to check their spam folder.

Q: What materials do I need to submit to accompany my application for admission to the Masters in Applied Data Science program?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Once I upload my unofficial transcripts to my application, do I still need to provide an official transcript?
A: Applicants must upload one unofficial transcript from each university attended. An unofficial undergraduate transcript is required even if the applicant holds advanced degrees. Transcripts should not be mailed for evaluation. If admitted, official transcripts from each university must be submitted before matriculation.

Q: If I am a student in the In-Person Master's in Applied Data Science Program, may I take courses in the Online Program? Conversely, if I am a student in the Online Program, may I take courses in the In-Person program?
A: Currently, students may only take courses in the modality in which they are officially enrolled.

Q: Do I need to be a US citizen or permanent resident to apply to Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program?
A: No. Students do not need to be U.S. citizens or permanent residents to participate in the Online Program. However, the Online Program is not eligible for visa sponsorship.

Q: How will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program impact my schedule? Are classes held synchronously, asynchronously, or both?
A: Classes generally take place on evenings and weekends. The Online Program is both synchronous and asynchronous. Students are required to participate in weekly live meetings with instructors and peers, complete readings and coursework, and engage in discussion.

Q: Will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program give me the opportunity to network with on-campus students, faculty/instructors, and advisors?
A: Yes. Online students are invited to an annual Immersion Weekend and may also access additional opportunities promoted by Career Services, such as virtual career fairs and advising/coaching appointments.

Q: What value do employers place on the Master's in Applied Data Science Online degree?
A: The page says employer value is significant, citing UChicago expectations for technical competence, communication, influence skills, and exposure to advanced data science technologies. It adds that the program’s theory-plus-application balance translates directly into practice.

Q: Is the Master's in Applied Data Science Online program equally academically rigorous as the In-Person program?
A: Yes. The Online curriculum is overseen by the same faculty curriculum committee as the In-Person program, and both are reviewed jointly and held to the same standards.

Q: Will my diploma indicate I completed the Master's in Applied Data Science Program Online?
A: No. The diploma does not include “Online” in the degree name.

Q: How do I apply to the MBA/MS joint degree program?
A: Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process. They complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application, select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest, and complete the MBA/MS supplement in the same application round.

Q: What courses will I take in the MBA/MS program?
A: Students take the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- Leadership Effectiveness and Development (LEAD)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

Booth courses are in person, while MS courses are online. Most students complete both degrees in seven quarters.

Q: Will MBA/MS courses be in-person or online?
A: Booth courses are in person, and MS courses are online. The program combines both formats for schedule flexibility.

Q: Are standardized tests required for admission?
A: Yes. Applicants must submit a GMAT or GRE score for the joint program. International applicants may also need proof of English proficiency through TOEFL iBT or IELTS. Listed minimums are TOEFL iBT 104 and IELTS 7, with waivers possible under certain criteria.

Q: What are the main differences in programs and outcomes between the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science compared to Computer Science?
A: The page says the MPCS program is centered on computer science areas such as software engineering, high-performance computing, data analytics, and application development. The MS-ADS program focuses on the intersection of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, including inference, modeling, machine learning, NLP, and deep learning. Example MS-ADS outcomes listed include Data Scientist, Senior Data Science Consultant, BI Director, Data Analytics Engineer, and AI Solution Architect.

2-YEAR THESIS TRACK PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the new 2-year thesis track program (18 courses), and how is it different from the 1-year program (12 courses)?
A: Beginning in academic year 2026–27, a limited number of in-person, full-time students may complete a 2-year version of the program over 21 months. Students complete 18 rather than 12 courses. The additional six courses include four extra electives and two required thesis courses that culminate in a written thesis or thesis project. The thesis extends the previously completed capstone project. The first 12 courses follow the same progression as the 1-year program. The 2-year option is not available to online or part-time students.

Q: When can I apply for the 2-year thesis track program?
A: The application for the Autumn 2026 2-Year Thesis Track closed on December 4, 2025. Future timelines will be announced later. Applicants not admitted to the 2-year program are automatically considered for the 1-year 12-course program unless they opt out.

Q: May I switch between the 1- and 2-year programs after officially enrolling in the MS-ADS program?
A: No. Students admitted to the 1-year program cannot later enroll in the 2-year program. Students admitted to the 2-year program may, in limited extenuating circumstances, graduate early after completing the initial 12 courses, subject to faculty/staff review and applicable deadlines.

Q: If I am admitted to the In-Person, Full-Time 1-year program, may I take any of the 2-year program electives?
A: No. Because of limited spots, those electives are reserved for students specifically admitted to the 2-year program.

Q: What is the 2-year program's thesis requirement?
A: Students in the full-time, in-person thesis-track pathway must complete and submit a written thesis based on an area of interest from their research capstone project. The thesis is managed through two required 100-unit thesis courses under faculty supervision. As an alternative, students may complete a thesis project resulting in a minimum viable product, also supervised by a faculty member.

Q: How many spots are available in the new 2-year program?
A: The page says there are projected to be about 50–55 spots, subject to change. For application year 2025–26 (for Autumn 2026 entry), applicants had to apply by the round two deadline of December 4, 2025, to be considered.

Letters of Recommendation
- Two letters of recommendation are required.
- The program strongly recommends that at least one letter be written by a direct manager/supervisor or internship supervisor who can attest to workplace skills such as leadership, teamwork, collaboration, initiative, and management.
- Letters of recommendation can be uploaded after the application is submitted.
- Recommended sources: professors, supervisors/managers, professional mentors.
- Letters from family members, friends, or peers are not accepted.

Candidate Statement
- The candidate statement is a key part of the application.
- The application includes a prompt with detailed instructions.
- Best practices:
  - Do not restate your resume.
  - Do not exceed 700 words.

Resume/CV
- The admissions committee reviews the resume carefully.
- Important to include:
  - Work experience, including internships and part-time positions in analytics/data science
  - Major accomplishments and awards
  - Educational background, including areas of study
  - Professional conferences attended
- Not needed:
  - Undergraduate or graduate GPA

Programming Supplement
- The application includes a section on programming skills.
- Applicants with limited skills in R or Python can still be accepted, but prior programming experience is valued.
- Submit a PDF of no more than two pages demonstrating current programming language skills.
- Example: a PDF of work completed in R or Python.
- The programming supplement must be shared as a PDF file.
- Example qualities they may look for in code:
  - Ingest data from csv
  - Manage different data types
  - Use your function for data analysis
  - Visualize data
  - Wrangle data
  - Write your own function
  - Import libraries

Virtual Portfolio
- Short recorded videos are required within the application.
- One video prompt response is required; another is optional.
- Each video should be approximately 1 minute 30 seconds.

International Students
- Only the full-time, in-person program is visa-eligible.
- International students may also apply to the online program, which does not require citizenship or permanent resident status and does not provide visa sponsorship.

English Language Requirement
- Applicants who do not meet the English Language Proficiency criteria must submit proof of English language proficiency.

  - IELTS: 7 (no subscore requirement)

Master’s in Applied Data Science FAQs

Overview:
The page presents frequently asked questions about the University of Chicago Master’s in Applied Data Science program.

------------------------------------------------------------
APPLICATION PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: When will I receive my Master's in Applied Data Science admission decision?
A: Admissions decisions are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Only completed applications are reviewed.

Q: If I finish my Master's in Applied Data Science application before the deadline, will I receive my decision early?
A: No. Admissions decisions for the in-person program are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Your application must be complete to be considered for review.

Q: How do I submit the materials that will accompany my Master's in Applied Data Science application?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Does the admissions office allow recommenders to email their letter directly as an attachment to be included in an applicant’s file?
A: No. Recommenders must upload their letter of support using the URL sent to them electronically by the online application system.

Q: Do I need to provide my recommenders with instructions?
A: No. Recommendation forms and instructions are sent electronically to recommenders once their names are entered within the online application.

Q: My recommender did not receive notification, can I resend it?
A: Yes. The applicant can resend the link through the online application or ask the recommender to check their spam folder.

Q: What materials do I need to submit to accompany my application for admission to the Masters in Applied Data Science program?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Once I upload my unofficial transcripts to my application, do I still need to provide an official transcript?
A: Applicants must upload one unofficial transcript from each university attended. An unofficial undergraduate transcript is required even if the applicant holds advanced degrees. Transcripts should not be mailed for evaluation. If admitted, official transcripts from each university must be submitted before matriculation.

Q: Is the GRE or GMAT required for the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: No. The GRE/GMAT is not required for admission.

Q: I took the GRE and/or GMAT and want to include my score(s) with my Master's in Applied Data Science application.
A: Scores may still be submitted. GRE school code: 1832. GMAT school code: H9X-WG-70.

Q: Who is exempt from providing proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the University of Chicago’s English Language Proficiency requirements.

Q: How will I be notified that I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: Applicants will be notified by email to check their application portal.

Q: If I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program, what do I do next?
A: Official e-transcripts should be sent to:
applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu

If the institution cannot send documents electronically, transcripts should be mailed to:
The University of Chicago
Attention: MS in Applied Data Science Admissions
455 N Cityfront Plaza Dr., Suite 2800
Chicago, Illinois 60611

Q: What test scores does UChicago accept as proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the official Proof of English Proficiency guidelines.

Q: What are the minimum scores required?
A: The page refers applicants to the Required Minimum Score guidelines.

Q: Where do I send my test scores?
A: TOEFL scores should be sent to the University of Chicago using the official instructions referenced on the page.

Q: I took the TOEFL over two years ago. Can I still use those TOEFL results?
A: The page refers applicants to the TOEFL validity guidelines.

Q: What’s the difference between the MS in Applied Data Science and the MS in Data Science programs at UChicago?
A: The page says both programs share a strong foundation in data science but differ in structure, location, and focus.
- The MS in Data Science is a 10-course program based in Hyde Park, includes a comprehensive research project, and is intended for students seeking deeper theoretical/research preparation.
- The MS in Applied Data Science offers a 12-course track and an 18-course thesis track, is available online and in person, and focuses on applying data science and machine learning to real-world problems through hands-on coursework, industry collaborations, and a two-quarter capstone or research project.

ONLINE PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: If I am a student in the In-Person Master's in Applied Data Science Program, may I take courses in the Online Program? Conversely, if I am a student in the Online Program, may I take courses in the In-Person program?
A: Currently, students may only take courses in the modality in which they are officially enrolled.

Q: Do I need to be a US citizen or permanent resident to apply to Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program?
A: No. Students do not need to be U.S. citizens or permanent residents to participate in the Online Program. However, the Online Program is not eligible for visa sponsorship.

Q: How will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program impact my schedule? Are classes held synchronously, asynchronously, or both?
A: Classes generally take place on evenings and weekends. The Online Program is both synchronous and asynchronous. Students are required to participate in weekly live meetings with instructors and peers, complete readings and coursework, and engage in discussion.

Q: Will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program give me the opportunity to network with on-campus students, faculty/instructors, and advisors?
A: Yes. Online students are invited to an annual Immersion Weekend and may also access additional opportunities promoted by Career Services, such as virtual career fairs and advising/coaching appointments.

Q: What value do employers place on the Master's in Applied Data Science Online degree?
A: The page says employer value is significant, citing UChicago expectations for technical competence, communication, influence skills, and exposure to advanced data science technologies. It adds that the program’s theory-plus-application balance translates directly into practice.

Q: Is the Master's in Applied Data Science Online program equally academically rigorous as the In-Person program?
A: Yes. The Online curriculum is overseen by the same faculty curriculum committee as the In-Person program, and both are reviewed jointly and held to the same standards.

Q: Will my diploma indicate I completed the Master's in Applied Data Science Program Online?
A: No. The diploma does not include “Online” in the degree name.

------------------------------------------------------------
MBA/MS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: How do I apply to the MBA/MS joint degree program?
A: Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process. They complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application, select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest, and complete the MBA/MS supplement in the same application round.

Q: What courses will I take in the MBA/MS program?
A: Students take the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- Leadership Effectiveness and Development (LEAD)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

Booth courses are in person, while MS courses are online. Most students complete both degrees in seven quarters.

Q: Will MBA/MS courses be in-person or online?
A: Booth courses are in person, and MS courses are online. The program combines both formats for schedule flexibility.

Q: Are standardized tests required for admission?
A: Yes. Applicants must submit a GMAT or GRE score for the joint program. International applicants may also need proof of English proficiency through TOEFL iBT or IELTS. Listed minimums are TOEFL iBT 104 and IELTS 7, with waivers possible under certain criteria.

Q: What are the main differences in programs and outcomes between the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science compared to Computer Science?
A: The page says the MPCS program is centered on computer science areas such as software engineering, high-performance computing, data analytics, and application development. The MS-ADS program focuses on the intersection of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, including inference, modeling, machine learning, NLP, and deep learning. Example MS-ADS outcomes listed include Data Scientist, Senior Data Science Consultant, BI Director, Data Analytics Engineer, and AI Solution Architect.

------------------------------------------------------------
2-YEAR THESIS TRACK PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the new 2-year thesis track program (18 courses), and how is it different from the 1-year program (12 courses)?
A: Beginning in academic year 2026–27, a limited number of in-person, full-time students may complete a 2-year version of the program over 21 months. Students complete 18 rather than 12 courses. The additional six courses include four extra electives and two required thesis courses that culminate in a written thesis or thesis project. The thesis extends the previously completed capstone project. The first 12 courses follow the same progression as the 1-year program. The 2-year option is not available to online or part-time students.

Q: When can I apply for the 2-year thesis track program?
A: The application for the Autumn 2026 2-Year Thesis Track closed on December 4, 2025. Future timelines will be announced later. Applicants not admitted to the 2-year program are automatically considered for the 1-year 12-course program unless they opt out.

Q: May I switch between the 1- and 2-year programs after officially enrolling in the MS-ADS program?
A: No. Students admitted to the 1-year program cannot later enroll in the 2-year program. Students admitted to the 2-year program may, in limited extenuating circumstances, graduate early after completing the initial 12 courses, subject to faculty/staff review and applicable deadlines.

Q: If I am admitted to the In-Person, Full-Time 1-year program, may I take any of the 2-year program electives?
A: No. Because of limited spots, those electives are reserved for students specifically admitted to the 2-year program.

Q: What is the 2-year program's thesis requirement?
A: Students in the full-time, in-person thesis-track pathway must complete and submit a written thesis based on an area of interest from their research capstone project. The thesis is managed through two required 100-unit thesis courses under faculty supervision. As an alternative, students may complete a thesis project resulting in a minimum viable product, also supervised by a faculty member.

Q: How many spots are available in the new 2-year program?
A: The page says there are projected to be about 50–55 spots, subject to change. For application year 2025–26 (for Autumn 2026 entry), applicants had to apply by the round two deadline of December 4, 2025, to be considered.

How to Apply

The application for entrance in 2026 is now open.

Master’s in Applied Data Science Application Requirements

- The application portal for 2026 entrance is open.
- The In-Person and Online program admits full- and part-time students for entrance in the autumn quarter annually.
- Only completed applications are sent for committee review.
- The application portal may close early if the cohort is filled.

Next Application Deadlines

In-Person — Autumn 2026
- January 26, 2026 — International Application Deadline (requiring visa sponsorship from UChicago) [passed]
- March 4, 2026 — Second Priority Application Deadline [passed]
- May 6, 2026 — Third Priority Application Deadline
- June 23, 2026 — Final Application Deadline

Online — Autumn 2026
- June 23, 2026 — Final Application Deadline

Important Notes
- International applicants who require a new visa and did not apply by the January 2026 deadline must plan to apply in the next application cycle for Autumn 2027 entry.
- Applicants currently studying in the United States on an F-1 visa and transferring their SEVIS record from another U.S. institution may still apply by the remaining application deadlines.

Letters of Recommendation
- Two letters of recommendation are required.
- The program strongly recommends that at least one letter be written by a direct manager/supervisor or internship supervisor who can attest to workplace skills such as leadership, teamwork, collaboration, initiative, and management.
- Letters of recommendation can be uploaded after the application is submitted.
- Recommended sources: professors, supervisors/managers, professional mentors.
- Letters from family members, friends, or peers are not accepted.

Candidate Statement
- The candidate statement is a key part of the application.
- The application includes a prompt with detailed instructions.
- Best practices:
  - Do not restate your resume.
  - Do not exceed 700 words.

Resume/CV
- The admissions committee reviews the resume carefully.
- Important to include:
  - Work experience, including internships and part-time positions in analytics/data science
  - Major accomplishments and awards
  - Educational background, including areas of study
  - Professional conferences attended
- Not needed:
  - Undergraduate or graduate GPA

Programming Supplement
- The application includes a section on programming skills.
- Applicants with limited skills in R or Python can still be accepted, but prior programming experience is valued.
- Submit a PDF of no more than two pages demonstrating current programming language skills.
- Example: a PDF of work completed in R or Python.
- The programming supplement must be shared as a PDF file.
- Example qualities they may look for in code:
  - Ingest data from csv
  - Manage different data types
  - Use your function for data analysis
  - Visualize data
  - Wrangle data
  - Write your own function
  - Import libraries

Virtual Portfolio
- Short recorded videos are required within the application.
- One video prompt response is required; another is optional.
- Each video should be approximately 1 minute 30 seconds.

International Students
- Only the full-time, in-person program is visa-eligible.
- International students may also apply to the online program, which does not require citizenship or permanent resident status and does not provide visa sponsorship.

English Language Requirement
- Applicants who do not meet the English Language Proficiency criteria must submit proof of English language proficiency.
- Minimum scores listed on the page:
  - TOEFL: 102 (prior requirement) or 5 (current requirement; no subscore requirement)
  - IELTS: 7 (no subscore requirement)
- Waiver policy is referenced on the University of Chicago Graduate Affairs page.

Transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended
- Only unofficial transcripts are required at the application stage.
- If admitted, official transcripts must be sent.
- A bachelor’s degree is required to apply.
- E-transcripts should be sent directly to:
  applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu
- If documents cannot be sent electronically, mail them to:
  The University of Chicago
  Attention: MS in Applied Data Science Admissions
  455 N Cityfront Plaza Dr., Suite 2800
  Chicago, Illinois 60611
- Mailed documents must arrive in the original, school-sealed envelope or they will be considered unofficial.
- If the transcript does not show degree conferral information, an attested copy of the degree certificate is also required.

Application Fee
- $90 non-refundable application fee.
- For fee waiver questions, refer to the Physical Sciences Division fee waiver policy.

------------------------------------------------------------
MBA/MS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: How do I apply to the MBA/MS joint degree program?
A: Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process. They complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application, select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest, and complete the MBA/MS supplement in the same application round.

Q: What courses will I take in the MBA/MS program?
A: Students take the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- Leadership Effectiveness and Development (LEAD)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

Booth courses are in person, while MS courses are online. Most students complete both degrees in seven quarters.

Q: Will MBA/MS courses be in-person or online?
A: Booth courses are in person, and MS courses are online. The program combines both formats for schedule flexibility.

Q: Are standardized tests required for admission?
A: Yes. Applicants must submit a GMAT or GRE score for the joint program. International applicants may also need proof of English proficiency through TOEFL iBT or IELTS. Listed minimums are TOEFL iBT 104 and IELTS 7, with waivers possible under certain criteria.

Q: What are the main differences in programs and outcomes between the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science compared to Computer Science?
A: The page says the MPCS program is centered on computer science areas such as software engineering, high-performance computing, data analytics, and application development. The MS-ADS program focuses on the intersection of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, including inference, modeling, machine learning, NLP, and deep learning. Example MS-ADS outcomes listed include Data Scientist, Senior Data Science Consultant, BI Director, Data Analytics Engineer, and AI Solution Architect.

------------------------------------------------------------
2-YEAR THESIS TRACK PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the new 2-year thesis track program (18 courses), and how is it different from the 1-year program (12 courses)?
A: Beginning in academic year 2026–27, a limited number of in-person, full-time students may complete a 2-year version of the program over 21 months. Students complete 18 rather than 12 courses. The additional six courses include four extra electives and two required thesis courses that culminate in a written thesis or thesis project. The thesis extends the previously completed capstone project. The first 12 courses follow the same progression as the 1-year program. The 2-year option is not available to online or part-time students.

Q: When can I apply for the 2-year thesis track program?
A: The application for the Autumn 2026 2-Year Thesis Track closed on December 4, 2025. Future timelines will be announced later. Applicants not admitted to the 2-year program are automatically considered for the 1-year 12-course program unless they opt out.

Q: May I switch between the 1- and 2-year programs after officially enrolling in the MS-ADS program?
A: No. Students admitted to the 1-year program cannot later enroll in the 2-year program. Students admitted to the 2-year program may, in limited extenuating circumstances, graduate early after completing the initial 12 courses, subject to faculty/staff review and applicable deadlines.

Q: If I am admitted to the In-Person, Full-Time 1-year program, may I take any of the 2-year program electives?
A: No. Because of limited spots, those electives are reserved for students specifically admitted to the 2-year program.

Q: What is the 2-year program's thesis requirement?
A: Students in the full-time, in-person thesis-track pathway must complete and submit a written thesis based on an area of interest from their research capstone project. The thesis is managed through two required 100-unit thesis courses under faculty supervision. As an alternative, students may complete a thesis project resulting in a minimum viable product, also supervised by a faculty member.

Q: How many spots are available in the new 2-year program?
A: The page says there are projected to be about 50–55 spots, subject to change. For application year 2025–26 (for Autumn 2026 entry), applicants had to apply by the round two deadline of December 4, 2025, to be considered.

Master’s in Applied Data Science FAQs

Overview:
The page presents frequently asked questions about the University of Chicago Master’s in Applied Data Science program.

------------------------------------------------------------
APPLICATION PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: When will I receive my Master's in Applied Data Science admission decision?
A: Admissions decisions are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Only completed applications are reviewed.

Q: If I finish my Master's in Applied Data Science application before the deadline, will I receive my decision early?
A: No. Admissions decisions for the in-person program are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Your application must be complete to be considered for review.

Q: How do I submit the materials that will accompany my Master's in Applied Data Science application?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Does the admissions office allow recommenders to email their letter directly as an attachment to be included in an applicant’s file?
A: No. Recommenders must upload their letter of support using the URL sent to them electronically by the online application system.

Q: Do I need to provide my recommenders with instructions?
A: No. Recommendation forms and instructions are sent electronically to recommenders once their names are entered within the online application.

Q: My recommender did not receive notification, can I resend it?
A: Yes. The applicant can resend the link through the online application or ask the recommender to check their spam folder.

Q: What materials do I need to submit to accompany my application for admission to the Masters in Applied Data Science program?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Once I upload my unofficial transcripts to my application, do I still need to provide an official transcript?
A: Applicants must upload one unofficial transcript from each university attended. An unofficial undergraduate transcript is required even if the applicant holds advanced degrees. Transcripts should not be mailed for evaluation. If admitted, official transcripts from each university must be submitted before matriculation.

Q: Is the GRE or GMAT required for the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: No. The GRE/GMAT is not required for admission.

Q: I took the GRE and/or GMAT and want to include my score(s) with my Master's in Applied Data Science application.
A: Scores may still be submitted. GRE school code: 1832. GMAT school code: H9X-WG-70.

Q: Who is exempt from providing proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the University of Chicago’s English Language Proficiency requirements.

Q: How will I be notified that I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: Applicants will be notified by email to check their application portal.

Q: If I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program, what do I do next?
A: Official e-transcripts should be sent to:
applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu

If the institution cannot send documents electronically, transcripts should be mailed to:
The University of Chicago
Attention: MS in Applied Data Science Admissions
455 N Cityfront Plaza Dr., Suite 2800
Chicago, Illinois 60611

Q: What test scores does UChicago accept as proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the official Proof of English Proficiency guidelines.

Q: What are the minimum scores required?
A: The page refers applicants to the Required Minimum Score guidelines.

Q: Where do I send my test scores?
A: TOEFL scores should be sent to the University of Chicago using the official instructions referenced on the page.

Q: I took the TOEFL over two years ago. Can I still use those TOEFL results?
A: The page refers applicants to the TOEFL validity guidelines.

Q: What’s the difference between the MS in Applied Data Science and the MS in Data Science programs at UChicago?
A: The page says both programs share a strong foundation in data science but differ in structure, location, and focus.
- The MS in Data Science is a 10-course program based in Hyde Park, includes a comprehensive research project, and is intended for students seeking deeper theoretical/research preparation.
- The MS in Applied Data Science offers a 12-course track and an 18-course thesis track, is available online and in person, and focuses on applying data science and machine learning to real-world problems through hands-on coursework, industry collaborations, and a two-quarter capstone or research project.

INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Which Master's in Applied Data Science provides students with visas?
A: The full-time In-Person 1-Year 12-Course Program and the 2-Year Thesis Track 18-Course Program are visa eligible.

Q: What is the total cost of tuition for the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: The page refers applicants to the Tuition, Fees, and Aid webpage.

A: The page refers applicants to the English Language Proficiency guidelines.

------------------------------------------------------------
ONLINE PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: If I am a student in the In-Person Master's in Applied Data Science Program, may I take courses in the Online Program? Conversely, if I am a student in the Online Program, may I take courses in the In-Person program?
A: Currently, students may only take courses in the modality in which they are officially enrolled.

Q: Do I need to be a US citizen or permanent resident to apply to Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program?
A: No. Students do not need to be U.S. citizens or permanent residents to participate in the Online Program. However, the Online Program is not eligible for visa sponsorship.

Q: How will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program impact my schedule? Are classes held synchronously, asynchronously, or both?
A: Classes generally take place on evenings and weekends. The Online Program is both synchronous and asynchronous. Students are required to participate in weekly live meetings with instructors and peers, complete readings and coursework, and engage in discussion.

Q: Will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program give me the opportunity to network with on-campus students, faculty/instructors, and advisors?
A: Yes. Online students are invited to an annual Immersion Weekend and may also access additional opportunities promoted by Career Services, such as virtual career fairs and advising/coaching appointments.

Q: What value do employers place on the Master's in Applied Data Science Online degree?
A: The page says employer value is significant, citing UChicago expectations for technical competence, communication, influence skills, and exposure to advanced data science technologies. It adds that the program’s theory-plus-application balance translates directly into practice.

Q: Is the Master's in Applied Data Science Online program equally academically rigorous as the In-Person program?
A: Yes. The Online curriculum is overseen by the same faculty curriculum committee as the In-Person program, and both are reviewed jointly and held to the same standards.

Q: Will my diploma indicate I completed the Master's in Applied Data Science Program Online?
A: No. The diploma does not include “Online” in the degree name.

------------------------------------------------------------
MBA/MS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: How do I apply to the MBA/MS joint degree program?
A: Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process. They complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application, select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest, and complete the MBA/MS supplement in the same application round.

Q: What courses will I take in the MBA/MS program?
A: Students take the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- Leadership Effectiveness and Development (LEAD)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

Booth courses are in person, while MS courses are online. Most students complete both degrees in seven quarters.

Q: Will MBA/MS courses be in-person or online?
A: Booth courses are in person, and MS courses are online. The program combines both formats for schedule flexibility.

Q: Are standardized tests required for admission?
A: Yes. Applicants must submit a GMAT or GRE score for the joint program. International applicants may also need proof of English proficiency through TOEFL iBT or IELTS. Listed minimums are TOEFL iBT 104 and IELTS 7, with waivers possible under certain criteria.

Q: What are the main differences in programs and outcomes between the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science compared to Computer Science?
A: The page says the MPCS program is centered on computer science areas such as software engineering, high-performance computing, data analytics, and application development. The MS-ADS program focuses on the intersection of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, including inference, modeling, machine learning, NLP, and deep learning. Example MS-ADS outcomes listed include Data Scientist, Senior Data Science Consultant, BI Director, Data Analytics Engineer, and AI Solution Architect.

------------------------------------------------------------
2-YEAR THESIS TRACK PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the new 2-year thesis track program (18 courses), and how is it different from the 1-year program (12 courses)?
A: Beginning in academic year 2026–27, a limited number of in-person, full-time students may complete a 2-year version of the program over 21 months. Students complete 18 rather than 12 courses. The additional six courses include four extra electives and two required thesis courses that culminate in a written thesis or thesis project. The thesis extends the previously completed capstone project. The first 12 courses follow the same progression as the 1-year program. The 2-year option is not available to online or part-time students.

Q: When can I apply for the 2-year thesis track program?
A: The application for the Autumn 2026 2-Year Thesis Track closed on December 4, 2025. Future timelines will be announced later. Applicants not admitted to the 2-year program are automatically considered for the 1-year 12-course program unless they opt out.

Q: May I switch between the 1- and 2-year programs after officially enrolling in the MS-ADS program?
A: No. Students admitted to the 1-year program cannot later enroll in the 2-year program. Students admitted to the 2-year program may, in limited extenuating circumstances, graduate early after completing the initial 12 courses, subject to faculty/staff review and applicable deadlines.

Q: If I am admitted to the In-Person, Full-Time 1-year program, may I take any of the 2-year program electives?
A: No. Because of limited spots, those electives are reserved for students specifically admitted to the 2-year program.

Q: What is the 2-year program's thesis requirement?
A: Students in the full-time, in-person thesis-track pathway must complete and submit a written thesis based on an area of interest from their research capstone project. The thesis is managed through two required 100-unit thesis courses under faculty supervision. As an alternative, students may complete a thesis project resulting in a minimum viable product, also supervised by a faculty member.

Q: How many spots are available in the new 2-year program?
A: The page says there are projected to be about 50–55 spots, subject to change. For application year 2025–26 (for Autumn 2026 entry), applicants had to apply by the round two deadline of December 4, 2025, to be considered.

Important Notes
- International applicants who require a new visa and did not apply by the January 2026 deadline must plan to apply in the next application cycle for Autumn 2027 entry.
- Applicants currently studying in the United States on an F-1 visa and transferring their SEVIS record from another U.S. institution may still apply by the remaining application deadlines.

Letters of Recommendation
- Two letters of recommendation are required.
- The program strongly recommends that at least one letter be written by a direct manager/supervisor or internship supervisor who can attest to workplace skills such as leadership, teamwork, collaboration, initiative, and management.
- Letters of recommendation can be uploaded after the application is submitted.
- Recommended sources: professors, supervisors/managers, professional mentors.
- Letters from family members, friends, or peers are not accepted.

Candidate Statement
- The candidate statement is a key part of the application.
- The application includes a prompt with detailed instructions.
- Best practices:
  - Do not restate your resume.
  - Do not exceed 700 words.

Resume/CV
- The admissions committee reviews the resume carefully.
- Important to include:
  - Work experience, including internships and part-time positions in analytics/data science
  - Major accomplishments and awards
  - Educational background, including areas of study
  - Professional conferences attended
- Not needed:
  - Undergraduate or graduate GPA

Programming Supplement
- The application includes a section on programming skills.
- Applicants with limited skills in R or Python can still be accepted, but prior programming experience is valued.
- Submit a PDF of no more than two pages demonstrating current programming language skills.
- Example: a PDF of work completed in R or Python.
- The programming supplement must be shared as a PDF file.
- Example qualities they may look for in code:
  - Ingest data from csv
  - Manage different data types
  - Use your function for data analysis
  - Visualize data
  - Wrangle data
  - Write your own function
  - Import libraries

Virtual Portfolio
- Short recorded videos are required within the application.
- One video prompt response is required; another is optional.
- Each video should be approximately 1 minute 30 seconds.

International Students
- Only the full-time, in-person program is visa-eligible.
- International students may also apply to the online program, which does not require citizenship or permanent resident status and does not provide visa sponsorship.

English Language Requirement
- Applicants who do not meet the English Language Proficiency criteria must submit proof of English language proficiency.
- Minimum scores listed on the page:
  - TOEFL: 102 (prior requirement) or 5 (current requirement; no subscore requirement)
  - IELTS: 7 (no subscore requirement)
- Waiver policy is referenced on the University of Chicago Graduate Affairs page.

Transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended
- Only unofficial transcripts are required at the application stage.
- If admitted, official transcripts must be sent.
- A bachelor’s degree is required to apply.

  applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu

  The University of Chicago

APPLICATION PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: When will I receive my Master's in Applied Data Science admission decision?
A: Admissions decisions are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Only completed applications are reviewed.

Q: If I finish my Master's in Applied Data Science application before the deadline, will I receive my decision early?
A: No. Admissions decisions for the in-person program are typically released 1–2 months after each application deadline. Your application must be complete to be considered for review.

Q: How do I submit the materials that will accompany my Master's in Applied Data Science application?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Does the admissions office allow recommenders to email their letter directly as an attachment to be included in an applicant’s file?
A: No. Recommenders must upload their letter of support using the URL sent to them electronically by the online application system.

Q: Do I need to provide my recommenders with instructions?
A: No. Recommendation forms and instructions are sent electronically to recommenders once their names are entered within the online application.

Q: My recommender did not receive notification, can I resend it?
A: Yes. The applicant can resend the link through the online application or ask the recommender to check their spam folder.

Q: What materials do I need to submit to accompany my application for admission to the Masters in Applied Data Science program?
A: The page directs applicants to review the How to Apply page.

Q: Once I upload my unofficial transcripts to my application, do I still need to provide an official transcript?
A: Applicants must upload one unofficial transcript from each university attended. An unofficial undergraduate transcript is required even if the applicant holds advanced degrees. Transcripts should not be mailed for evaluation. If admitted, official transcripts from each university must be submitted before matriculation.

Q: Is the GRE or GMAT required for the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: No. The GRE/GMAT is not required for admission.

Q: I took the GRE and/or GMAT and want to include my score(s) with my Master's in Applied Data Science application.
A: Scores may still be submitted. GRE school code: 1832. GMAT school code: H9X-WG-70.

Q: Who is exempt from providing proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the University of Chicago’s English Language Proficiency requirements.

Q: How will I be notified that I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program?
A: Applicants will be notified by email to check their application portal.

Q: If I am admitted to the Master's in Applied Data Science program, what do I do next?
A: Official e-transcripts should be sent to:
applieddatascience-admissions@uchicago.edu

If the institution cannot send documents electronically, transcripts should be mailed to:
The University of Chicago
Attention: MS in Applied Data Science Admissions
455 N Cityfront Plaza Dr., Suite 2800
Chicago, Illinois 60611

Q: What test scores does UChicago accept as proof of English proficiency?
A: The page refers applicants to the official Proof of English Proficiency guidelines.

Q: What are the minimum scores required?
A: The page refers applicants to the Required Minimum Score guidelines.

Q: Where do I send my test scores?
A: TOEFL scores should be sent to the University of Chicago using the official instructions referenced on the page.

Q: I took the TOEFL over two years ago. Can I still use those TOEFL results?
A: The page refers applicants to the TOEFL validity guidelines.

Q: What’s the difference between the MS in Applied Data Science and the MS in Data Science programs at UChicago?
A: The page says both programs share a strong foundation in data science but differ in structure, location, and focus.
- The MS in Data Science is a 10-course program based in Hyde Park, includes a comprehensive research project, and is intended for students seeking deeper theoretical/research preparation.
- The MS in Applied Data Science offers a 12-course track and an 18-course thesis track, is available online and in person, and focuses on applying data science and machine learning to real-world problems through hands-on coursework, industry collaborations, and a two-quarter capstone or research project.

ONLINE PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: If I am a student in the In-Person Master's in Applied Data Science Program, may I take courses in the Online Program? Conversely, if I am a student in the Online Program, may I take courses in the In-Person program?
A: Currently, students may only take courses in the modality in which they are officially enrolled.

Q: Do I need to be a US citizen or permanent resident to apply to Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program?
A: No. Students do not need to be U.S. citizens or permanent residents to participate in the Online Program. However, the Online Program is not eligible for visa sponsorship.

Q: How will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program impact my schedule? Are classes held synchronously, asynchronously, or both?
A: Classes generally take place on evenings and weekends. The Online Program is both synchronous and asynchronous. Students are required to participate in weekly live meetings with instructors and peers, complete readings and coursework, and engage in discussion.

Q: Will enrolling in Master's in Applied Data Science Online Program give me the opportunity to network with on-campus students, faculty/instructors, and advisors?
A: Yes. Online students are invited to an annual Immersion Weekend and may also access additional opportunities promoted by Career Services, such as virtual career fairs and advising/coaching appointments.

Q: What value do employers place on the Master's in Applied Data Science Online degree?
A: The page says employer value is significant, citing UChicago expectations for technical competence, communication, influence skills, and exposure to advanced data science technologies. It adds that the program’s theory-plus-application balance translates directly into practice.

Q: Is the Master's in Applied Data Science Online program equally academically rigorous as the In-Person program?
A: Yes. The Online curriculum is overseen by the same faculty curriculum committee as the In-Person program, and both are reviewed jointly and held to the same standards.

Q: Will my diploma indicate I completed the Master's in Applied Data Science Program Online?
A: No. The diploma does not include “Online” in the degree name.

------------------------------------------------------------
MBA/MS
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: How do I apply to the MBA/MS joint degree program?
A: Applicants apply through Booth’s centralized joint-application process. They complete the Chicago Booth Full-Time MBA application, select the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science as the program of interest, and complete the MBA/MS supplement in the same application round.

Q: What courses will I take in the MBA/MS program?
A: Students take the equivalent of 23 100-unit courses:
- 14 MBA classes
- 9 data science courses
- Leadership Effectiveness and Development (LEAD)
- Qualified Work Experience (noncredit professional internship experience)

Booth courses are in person, while MS courses are online. Most students complete both degrees in seven quarters.

Q: Will MBA/MS courses be in-person or online?
A: Booth courses are in person, and MS courses are online. The program combines both formats for schedule flexibility.

Q: Are standardized tests required for admission?
A: Yes. Applicants must submit a GMAT or GRE score for the joint program. International applicants may also need proof of English proficiency through TOEFL iBT or IELTS. Listed minimums are TOEFL iBT 104 and IELTS 7, with waivers possible under certain criteria.

Q: What are the main differences in programs and outcomes between the MBA/MS in Applied Data Science compared to Computer Science?
A: The page says the MPCS program is centered on computer science areas such as software engineering, high-performance computing, data analytics, and application development. The MS-ADS program focuses on the intersection of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, including inference, modeling, machine learning, NLP, and deep learning. Example MS-ADS outcomes listed include Data Scientist, Senior Data Science Consultant, BI Director, Data Analytics Engineer, and AI Solution Architect.

------------------------------------------------------------
2-YEAR THESIS TRACK PROGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the new 2-year thesis track program (18 courses), and how is it different from the 1-year program (12 courses)?
A: Beginning in academic year 2026–27, a limited number of in-person, full-time students may complete a 2-year version of the program over 21 months. Students complete 18 rather than 12 courses. The additional six courses include four extra electives and two required thesis courses that culminate in a written thesis or thesis project. The thesis extends the previously completed capstone project. The first 12 courses follow the same progression as the 1-year program. The 2-year option is not available to online or part-time students.

Q: When can I apply for the 2-year thesis track program?
A: The application for the Autumn 2026 2-Year Thesis Track closed on December 4, 2025. Future timelines will be announced later. Applicants not admitted to the 2-year program are automatically considered for the 1-year 12-course program unless they opt out.

Q: May I switch between the 1- and 2-year programs after officially enrolling in the MS-ADS program?
A: No. Students admitted to the 1-year program cannot later enroll in the 2-year program. Students admitted to the 2-year program may, in limited extenuating circumstances, graduate early after completing the initial 12 courses, subject to faculty/staff review and applicable deadlines.

Q: If I am admitted to the In-Person, Full-Time 1-year program, may I take any of the 2-year program electives?
A: No. Because of limited spots, those electives are reserved for students specifically admitted to the 2-year program.

Q: What is the 2-year program's thesis requirement?
A: Students in the full-time, in-person thesis-track pathway must complete and submit a written thesis based on an area of interest from their research capstone project. The thesis is managed through two required 100-unit thesis courses under faculty supervision. As an alternative, students may complete a thesis project resulting in a minimum viable product, also supervised by a faculty member.

Q: How many spots are available in the new 2-year program?
A: The page says there are projected to be about 50–55 spots, subject to change. For application year 2025–26 (for Autumn 2026 entry), applicants had to apply by the round two deadline of December 4, 2025, to be considered.
